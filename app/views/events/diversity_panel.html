Diversity in InfoSec Panel Discussion

On Wednesday, February 1, we hosted a live chat with several conference organizers talking about some of the questions that many first-time speakers have.

Moderator

Lisa Weeks @XenoGirl


Panelists

Elena Steinke

Steinke is a Director at WSC, a nonprofit dedicated to promoting women in Cybersecurity. Steinke is a sought after industry recognized leader in Cybersecurity, and overall Systems Engineering, with experience that includes developing solutions, providing strategic direction, establishing and directing cyber security operations. Her work impacts Cybersecurity operations across continents on multi-national projects, in the financial sector, and within the US Federal space Steinke stood up and directed multiple Network Operation Centers, Security Operation Centers, Data Centers that included managing the development of virtualized storage technologies, and elastic cloud environments, to include Governance, Compliance & Policy, Risk Management, Vulnerability Management, Security Assessment, and Pen Testing programs across industries Steinke holds an undergraduate degree in IT, and an MBA. Certifications include CISSP, CEHv9, and Security+.


Kate Shackford

Kate Shackford, ICMCP Director of Programs, has over thirty years’ nonprofit experience. She spent thirteen years working in environmental justice communities in the Bronx through GRID Alternatives Tri- State as Executive Director and the Bronx Overall Economic Development Corporation as Executive Vice President and Director of the Bronx Initiative for Energy and the Environment.


David Elcock @devidelcock

David Elcock comes to The International Consortium of Minority Cybersecurity Professionals as the Chief Corporate Development Officer and co-founder of Lynx Technology Partners. David’s track record spans three generations as a proven convener and team builder in both for-profit and non-profit enterprises. In the non-profit sector, David has held a variety of positions from General Manager to Board with The Enterprise Foundation, National Equity Fund, Access One Broadband, One Economy, Mount Hope Housing Company, Morris Heights Healthcare Center, Food for Survival and World Share.
