Brainstorming and Research Methods

How to brainstorm an idea and how to flesh it out a bit, and then break off into groups to come up with potential research topics. Next, how to take your research question and then create good tests and methods. This session will be sign up and walk in, expecting a max of 100 participants.

Estimated: 50 minutes


Submitting a CFP Workshop

Committee members from different BSides CFP's will talk about their particular requirements for CFP and their selection process. Then, CFP committee members and experienced speakers will help attendees who came to this session to write mock papers for practice. Participants are encouraged to bring a project they've done in the past or a new idea and write a CFP as if they were going to submit it. Attendance will be based upon sign up ahead of time and limited to 30 participants to allow for smaller workshop groups.

Estimated: 110 minutes


Making the Presentation

This session will work on presentation techniques from how to set up your power point and not put code snippets and math on your slides, how to keep people engaged, and how to explain complex topics in a way that most people in your audience will understand. This session will be sign up and walk in, expecting a max of 100 participants.

Estimated: 50 minutes


How to Encourage Submissions and Participation from Diverse Groups

This will be a round table discussion on setting up a code of conduct, doing blind review, and general discussion about bringing greater diversity to conferences. Attendance will be based upon sign up ahead of time and limited to 15 participants to keep the conversation controlled while allowing as many people as possible time to talk.

This session is only for conference programmers.

Estimated: 50 minutes
